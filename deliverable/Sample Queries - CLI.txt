You: What happens if an agency overchages a worker?
Question: What happens if an agency overchages a worker?
Answer: If an employment agency overcharges a job-seeker (including a worker) commission, it is a serious offence. Offenders will be prosecuted and are liable to a fine of **$350,000** and imprisonment for **3 years**. Upon conviction, the Commissioner for Labour will refuse to renew or will revoke the agency's licence.

**Source:** Context, paragraph under "Overcharging job-seekers commission is a serious offence."

Question: Can Foreign worker live outside their employer's residence?
Answer: Based on the provided context, a foreign domestic helper (FDH) generally cannot live away from their employer's home.
